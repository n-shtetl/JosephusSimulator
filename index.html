Josephus Simulator
During the first Roman-Jewish War, historian Flavius Josephus and his fellow soldiers were besieged by Roman forces. They withdrew into a cave, and probably apocryphally, drew up a method of killing themselves to avoid the humiliation of surrender. According to Josephus, they arranged themselves in a circle and cut down every other person until there was only one left. Josephus calculated what position he needed to be in to be the sole survivor.

The generalized, abstracted version of this problem is a common introductory problem in CS and discrete math courses. The first step in finding a general solution (1≤k≤n) is playing with different values of n and k.
